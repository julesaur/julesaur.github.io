Four Stages of Life |Premiere Pro, Touch Designer, Canva, & SNOW| 2020

I divided the video into four different video short chapters in the order of a person’s life preferably in today’s society. Each video has its own style in terms of visual purposes that complement the title. Making this video got me thinking about the common stages that some people could have gone through including me as they might have to experience into the unknown world, acted as a rebel or spoiled, appreciating who we love, and finally hearing words from people with wisdom.

Innocence ch. 1

“Innocence” is about a baby being born on Earth with no knowledge, but with pure curiosity. The color white, the moon, and the baby symbolizes innocence. As for the text, I created a poem expressing the qualities of an innocent child and how long it is acceptable to remain ignorant.

Dependence ch. 2

“Dependence” where I filmed a conversation with Siri on an iPhone. This chapter is a rebellious and spoiled stage after “Innocence”. Not only I used Siri has my main point, but I also added a typewriter effect on Premiere Pro as another “person” to rely on which is a search bar engine. This suggested that every time I typed something, I would find direct answers immediately. In the video, it is a short parody of my conversation with Siri. Every time I demanded something, Siri replies, “I’m not sure I understand”. After every consistent of her response, I swore at the AI for being useless. I viewed “Dependence” as relying on technology and change of behavior for being too dependent on it.

Luvv ch. 3

“Luvv” is about exploring words that people called love. I changed the spelling to create my own definition of “Love” since it does not define only one relationship. I complied with the most commons words that can be call love. Note that every word listed may not satisfy every viewer, but it is how I always hear. I want my viewers to read positive words that stand out to them rather than controversial ones.

Motto ch. 4

I think of the "Motto" as the last stage because it reminds me of a piece of advice given by a wise person. A wise person sounds like someone you look up to who achieved greater things in their life. That’s why I have the image of a palm offering mottos to someone who hasn’t experienced anything yet. The motto came from the words that I made up as a motivation in this challenging world, I think it is a beautiful piece that I hope the audience will be able to take away from.
